VCs Flock to Early-Stage CyberSecurity Startups

Cybersecurity is a red-hot investment area, and venture capitalists are looking for good deals. That seems to be getting in on Seed rounds and Series A financing.

By Fahmida Y Rashid

Published July 20, 2019

Security startups shooting up left and right and it seems like there's plenty of money to go around. With CrowdStrike IPOing at $34 and hitting $100 a share in less than 2 months, and Duo Security nearing its one-year anniversary of being acquired by Cisco for $2.35 billion, cybersecurity is a booming business.

An analysis of Crunchbase figures for cybersecurity startups--which includes companies that identify as cybersecurity, privacy, compliance, network, cloud security, identity management--found that while security investments continued to be strong over the fast 24 months, they have been on decli

Deals Down in 2019 from 2018

The first two quarters of 2018 was particularly good for startups trying to raise capital, since VCs were on a tear making deals. Since then the number of deals closed that year has declined.

Sources: Crunchbase

Early vs Late Stage Financing

While there is a decline in how many funding deals are being sighend on a quarter-by-quarter basis, the excitement is still there. VCs are still taking part in Series A-G rounds. While VCs continue to help launch a company with _seed_ funding,most of them appear to be willing to take a slower approach and wait for the startups to grow up a little bit more.

The median size of the funding deal shows that regardless of the type of financial round, startups will not be hurting for funds and investors. 2018 seems to be an outlier, with more deals. The median funding shows that both 2017 and 2019 had very similar amound of funds invested.

Over the last 24 months, Angel and Pre-Seed rounding tend to be smaller sums of money, but about the same across the years. Series A and B is where the investors pull out the bigger guns.

Looking at the Early Days

Investors are flocking to startups' early rounds of financing. Series A and Series B are more popular rounds of investing than any of the other series.

Sources: Crunchbase

While the number of deals may be declining overall, activity is high for Series A financing, confirming that VCs are looking at earlier stage companies rather than maturing one.

Series A Going Strong

Startups looking to raise Series A capital would likely not have trouble finding investors.

Source: Crunchbase
