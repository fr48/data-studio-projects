Brace Yourself: More Data Breaches in 2019

If you are tired of hearing about data breaches, there's some bad news: More people will be impacted by data breaches this year than last year.

By Fahmida Rashid

Published July 16, 2019

2019 is shaping up to be a rough year for Massachusetts residents when it comes to their information being stolen or exposed online.

More Massachusetts residents had their information stolen or somehow exposed online in the first six months of 2019 than in the first half of 2018. With the current pace of data breaches, it is very likely the total number of affected victims in 2019 will exceed 2018.

In an analysis of data breaches that affected Massachusetts residents in 2018 and 2019, it was readily apparent that data breaches weren't limit to only certain types of organizations or industry sectors. The fact that personal information for thousands of people--Social Security numbers, account information, payment card details, and driver's license data--are falling into unauthorized hands is a widespread problem. The top 10 largest data breaches (in terms of number of Massachusetts residents affected) in 2019 included healthcare (Doctors' Management Services), education (Georgia Institute of Technology), government (Oklahoma Department of Securities; Massachusetts Department of Transportation), and financial (collections agency Retrieval-Masters Creditors Bureau).
